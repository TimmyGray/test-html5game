## What Went Well

- **Single timing authority held:** Effects and scheduling remained tied to `SyncClock` / audio timeline across all stories.
- **One trigger pipeline stayed intact:** Deflection confirmation path (`pickBestStormHit` / `EVENTS.ASTEROID_HIT`) remained the authoritative seam for impulse + VFX + SFX.
- **Pure logic extracted and tested:** Beat phase math, spectacle envelopes, slow-mo windows, and quantized SFX logic all received focused unit tests.
- **Performance guardrails were respected:** Hot-path allocation discipline, branch-light shader paths, and profile-aware notes appeared consistently.
- **Review loops improved quality:** Follow-up passes closed meaningful issues (audio-time stall handling, monotonic beat ticks, deterministic burst RNG, scheduling guards).
- **Scope boundaries were explicit:** Story boundaries (2.2 vs 2.3 vs 2.4) reduced overlap and prevented feature bleed.

---

## Challenges and Patterns

- **Bootstrap orchestration is getting heavy:** `bootstrap-gameplay.ts` and `main.ts` now coordinate many systems and risk becoming brittle if Epic 3 expands similarly.
- **Audio edge conditions are persistent:** Non-advancing/backward audio-time cases required extra hardening and are likely to recur.
- **Viewport/input coupling surfaced again:** Tap-center and stage/renderer coordinate assumptions needed correction and tests.
- **Shader path hardening is not fully closed:** Uniform/packing concerns were deferred and should be validated before larger Epic 3 shader additions.
- **Manual cross-backend QA remains essential:** WebGPU vs WebGL and "feel" checks continue to complement unit tests.

## Next Epic Preview (Epic 3)

**Epic 3 Goal:** Challenge, progression, and fail-state bridge (`health`, `combo`, `gold debris`, `intensity FSM`).

### Dependencies from Epic 2

- Stable timing/rhythm core under time scaling and audio edge cases.
- Existing hit-resolution seam for authoritative gameplay side effects.
- Existing shader/filter infrastructure requiring careful extension, not ad-hoc layering.

### Significant Discoveries for Epic 3 Readiness

1. **Uniform contract planning is mandatory:** Epic 3 health/intensity visuals should extend a coherent shader uniform model.
2. **Time-scale interactions must stay deterministic:** Combo/health/FSM transitions must behave correctly during slow-mo and audio stalls.
3. **Single authoritative gameplay seam must be preserved:** Damage, combo resets, and rewards should not branch into competing logic paths.
4. **Module-boundary pressure is rising:** Further orchestration growth in bootstrap should be prevented with clearer subsystem ownership.
