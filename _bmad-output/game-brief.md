# Game Brief: The Reactive Planet

**Date:** 2016-04-08
**Author:** Timmy
**Status:** Complete - Ready for GDD/Technical Spec

---

## Executive Summary

*The Reactive Planet* is a premium HTML5 playable ad designed as a high-performance technical showcase for marketing development portfolios.

**Target Audience:** Technical Recruiters and Marketing Leads at top-tier mobile studios seeking Senior-level frontend mastery.

**Core Pillars:**
- **Visual Smash:** Custom PixiJS shaders for every interaction.
- **Butter-Smooth Response:** Sub-millisecond input handling at a locked 60fps.
- **The "One-Verb" Rule:** Instant mastery through a single, intuitive flick action.

**Key Differentiators:** Hand-crafted GLSL shaders (no generic plugins), Rhythmic Atmospheric Synergy, and a "Mini-Masterpiece" standard for every collision.

**Platform:** Mobile and Desktop Web (PixiJS 8 + Webpack 5).

**Success Vision:** A 15-second interactive masterclass that demonstrates the technical proficiency required to secure a Senior Marketing Developer position.

---

## Success Criteria

### MVP Definition

- **Core Loop:** Single asteroid type with velocity-sensitive "flick" interaction and trajectory trails.
- **Planetoid:** Pulsing central planet with atmospheric heartbeat and displacement-filter shockwaves.
- **Failure State:** High-performance physically-accurate "Shatter" debris explosion.
- **Optimization:** Locked 60fps on mobile and desktop browsers using PixiJS 8's WebGL/WebGPU pipeline.

### Success Metrics

- **Technical Validation:** Lighthouse Performance score > 90 on mobile profiles.
- **Professional Impact:** Direct technical inquiries from potential employers regarding shader implementation or optimized build-pipeline.
- **User Satisfaction:** Immediate "Wow" factor and positive feedback on "Juice" and "Game Feel" during initial playback.

## Game Vision

### Core Concept

A highly reactive orbital defense experience where you flick debris away from a pulsing planetoid, focusing on premium 'juice' and rhythmic feedback.

### Elevator Pitch

The Reactive Planet is a premium HTML5 playable creative designed as a technical showcase for marketing portfolios. Players flick incoming asteroids away from a central world, triggering musical scales and rhythmic atmospheric distortions using PixiJS filters. It’s a 15-second masterclass in 'juice' and responsive game feel that demonstrates high-end mobile web performance.

### Vision Statement

To create a 15-second interactive experience so polished and reactive that it feels like a physical object in the player's hands, proving that even the simplest interaction can be a beautiful, high-performance technical feat.

## Game Fundamentals

### Core Gameplay Pillars

- **Visual Smash:** Every interaction (flicks, collisions, pulses) must be a **"mini-masterpiece"** of visual payoff, utilizing **custom PixiJS shaders**, displacement maps, and high-fidelity particle systems.
- **Butter-Smooth Response:** Sub-millisecond input-to-render feedback, maintaining a locked 60fps to demonstrate "Senior-level" technical optimization.
- **The "One-Verb" Rule:** Balancing deep visual complexity with a single, intuitive "Flick" action for zero learning curve and instant professional-grade satisfaction.

**Pillar Priority:** When pillars conflict, prioritize:
1. **Visual Smash** (The core hook)
2. **Butter-Smooth Response** (The technical standard)
3. **The "One-Verb" Rule** (The accessibility)

### Primary Mechanics

- **Velocity-Sensitive High-Fidelity Flick:** Players flick tiles/asteroids away from the planet. The speed of the swipe uses a high-fidelity input buffer to calculate precise force, trail length, and shader intensity.
- **Atmospheric Rhythmic Pulse:** The central planet pulses with a "living" heartbeat, triggering custom displacement-filter shockwaves and rhythmic atmospheric distortions.
- **Professional Physics Shatter:** Catastrophic failure triggers a physically-accurate shattering of the planet into a cloud of high-performance fragments, showcasing custom collision and debris logic.

**Core Loop:** **Flick (Precise Input) → Smash (Visual Payoff) → Defend (Rhythmic Flow).** Repeat as the shader intensity and pulse frequency increase until the final climax or a beautiful failure.

### Player Experience Goals

- **Senior-Level Professionalism:** The recruiter should immediately recognize the "custom-built" nature of the VFX and performance (no generic plugins), separating this as "Master-level" work.
- **Tactile Mastery:** A "Zen-like" flow where the flick feels physically weighty and the visual payoff is addictive.

**Emotional Journey:** Surprise (Beauty) → Focus (Challenge) → Awe (The Smash) → **Professional Respect** (VFX Quality).

## Content Framework

### World and Setting

The world of *The Reactive Planet* is a minimalist, hypnotic sci-fi void. The central pulsing planetoid is a highly stylized 2D object set against a deep nebula of neon gradients and glowing cosmic dust. The setting exists primarily to showcase **custom-coded atmospheric effects** and **high-performance orbital physics**.

### Narrative Approach

**Minimal/Environmental.** The narrative is told through the planet's rhythmic "heartbeat" and the intensity of the incoming orbital debris. 15-30 seconds of pure, wordless interaction.

**Story Delivery:** Purely visual and auditory—distortions, color shifts, and bass-heavy feedback.

### Content Volume

A single, masterfully polished level (15-30 seconds) designed for immediate replayability through addictive feedback loops.

---

## Art and Audio Direction

### Visual Style

A clean, modern, and stylized 2D aesthetic. High contrast with **neon-soaked colors** (pinks, purples, cyans). The "Visual Smash" is sold through **Advanced Shaders**: displacement maps for shockwaves, glow filters for projectiles, and chromatic aberration on impacts.

**References:** *Hyper Light Drifter* (Color Palette), *Rez Infinite* (Rhythmic Geometry).

### Audio Style

**Rhythmic Synth-Pulse.** A heavy, atmospheric bass-pulse that represents the planet's "heartbeat" and the game's tempo. Sound effects for "The Smash" are designed to be "ear candy"—satisfying, bass-heavy, and synced to the visual distortions.

### Production Approach

**Hybrid Optimization.** We will utilize high-quality **Open Source assets** for base geometry and audio, but we will "up-cycle" them using **custom PixiJS 8.0 filters** and unique shader logic to prove professional-grade mastery.

## Risk Assessment

### Key Risks

- **Technical Plan Fragility (High Impact):** Building without a rock-solid architectural plan leading to "spaghetti code" or performance bottlenecks.
- **Skill Gap Lag (Medium Impact):** Potential delays caused by the transition to PixiJS 8 or complex Webpack configuration.
- **Optimization Failure (Medium Impact):** High-fidelity filters causing frame drops on mid-range mobile browsers.

### Technical Challenges

The primary challenge is the orchestration of multiple custom shaders (displacement + glow + bloom) in a single render loop while maintaining 60fps on mobile.

### Mitigation Strategies

- **Comprehensive Technical Spec:** Before any gameplay coding, we will move to a **GDS Quick-Spec phase** to lock in the PixiJS 8/Webpack pipeline and the Shader Architecture.
- **Shader Profiling:** We will profile the GPU/CPU impact of every custom shader in real-time to ensure 60fps compliance.
- **Atomic Implementation:** We will build the "Flick" and "Smash" as modular, performant systems before layering on the heavy post-processing effects.
